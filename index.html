Frequently Asked Questions & Grading Information

SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average) and TGPA (Term Grade Point Average) measure a student's academic performance in a single semester/term. It is calculated by dividing the sum of the credit points earned in all courses by the total number of credits registered.

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) measures a student's cumulative academic performance across all completed semesters in a program. It is computed as the weighted average of all semesters' SGPA/TGPA based on their respective credits.

Lovely Professional University (LPU) uses Relative Grading for most courses. Rather than matching absolute mark ranges (like 90%+ is an A), grade cutoffs are computed based on the cohort's average marks and standard deviation.

If an exam is extremely difficult, the class average goes down, and a student scoring 70% might still receive an 'O' (Outstanding) or 'A+' grade. Conversely, in a very easy exam where the average is 90%, scoring 85% might result in a lower grade like 'B'. The grade point values, however, remain fixed (O = 10, A+ = 9, A = 8, etc.).

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Technical University (AKTU) uses a specific formula to convert CGPA to percentage, defined by their academic council:

Percentage (%) = (CGPA × 10) - 7.5

For example, if your CGPA is 8.25 at AKTU, your equivalent percentage is (8.25 × 10) - 7.5 = 75.00%.

Most universities under UGC CBCS require a minimum Grade Point of 4.0 (Grade D or P) in each subject to pass. A grade point of 0 (E, F, or U) is considered a fail or reappear paper.

To graduate with **First Class with Distinction** or **Honours**, universities like AKTU and LPU generally require maintaining a CGPA of 7.5 or above without any carryover papers/backlogs during the entire program.
